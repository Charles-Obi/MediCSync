Departments

Here Are Some of Our Departments

  • Out Patient Department (OPD)
  • Ophthalmology Department
  • Laboratory Services Department
  • Physiotherapy Department
  • Obstetrics and Gynecology Department

Out Patient Department (OPD)

Where patients receive medical consultations, diagnostic services, and minor treatments without requiring admission or an overnight stay.

Consultations: Patients meet with doctors across various specialties for diagnosis and treatment planning.

Diagnostics: Includes services such as blood tests, X-rays, ultrasounds, and other diagnostic procedures.

Follow-Ups: Patients return for post-treatment or post-surgery evaluations.

Ophthalmology Department

Dedicated to the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and conditions affecting the eyes and vision.

Eye Examinations: Comprehensive eye exams to assess vision, detect refractive errors (like myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism), and screen for eye diseases.

Emergency Eye Care: Treatment of acute eye injuries, infections, and conditions like conjunctivitis or foreign body removal.

Diagnosis and Treatment: Diagnosis and medical management of conditions such as cataracts, glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, and retinal disorders.

Laboratory Services Department

An integral part of the healthcare system, providing critical diagnostic information that assists healthcare professionals in making accurate diagnoses and treatment decisions.

Clinical Chemistry:Analyzes blood and urine samples to measure levels of enzymes, proteins, hormones, glucose, and other substances.

Immunology:Performs tests to measure immune responses, diagnose autoimmune diseases, and detect infectious agents or allergens.

Physiotherapy Department

Is responsible for helping patients recover from physical injuries, surgeries, and various medical conditions that affect movement and physical function.

Sports Physiotherapy:Focuses on the rehabilitation of athletes and active individuals with sports-related injuries, aiming to restore performance and prevent future injuries.

Orthopedic Physiotherapy: Focuses on conditions related to the bones, joints, and soft tissues (e.g., fractures, sprains, arthritis, and post-surgical rehabilitation).

Obstetrics and Gynecology Department

Dedicated to providing medical care related to the female reproductive system, including the management of pregnancy, childbirth, and conditions affecting the female organs such as the uterus, ovaries, and vagina.

Labor and Delivery: Management of labor, including natural childbirth, Caesarean sections (C-sections), and any emergency procedures that may arise during delivery.

Menstrual Disorders: Diagnosis and treatment of conditions related to irregular periods, heavy bleeding, or absence of menstruation.

Fibroid Removal: Surgical removal of fibroids that cause significant pain, bleeding, or other complications.
